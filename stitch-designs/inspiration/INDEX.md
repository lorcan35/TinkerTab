## Design Themes to Extract for Glyph OS

### Color Palette References
- **Linear** (#19, #20): Warm dark grays, not cold blue-blacks. High contrast white text.
- **eDEX-UI** (#13-16): Neon accent colors (cyan, orange, pink) on pure black.
- **Nothing OS** (#21-27): Pure AMOLED black with white dot-matrix typography.
- **Cyberpunk** (#17-18): Neon blue/green data visualization on dark charcoal.

### Layout Patterns
- **Card stacks** (Rabbit R1 #1-6, Humane #32): Swipeable card navigation for a small touchscreen.
- **Widget grids** (Nothing OS #24, #27): 2x2 and 4x4 widget grids on lock/home screens.
- **Dashboard panels** (eDEX #13-16, Smart Home #28): Multi-panel information density.
- **Minimal controls** (Humane #29-31): Extreme constraint -- fewest possible UI elements.

### Typography
- **Nothing's dot-matrix font** (#22-25): Monospaced, retro-futuristic, highly legible on dark.
- **Linear's warm grays** (#19-20): Professional, clean, high-contrast sans-serif.
- **TE-inspired monospace** (#7-12): Industrial, functional, Teenage Engineering aesthetic.

### Animation/Motion
- **Humane rolodex** (#32): Depth-based card flip transitions.
- **Humane projection** (#33): Content materializing/dissolving.
- **eDEX glitch** (#15): Glitch effects for personality/mood states.

### Key Takeaways for Glyph OS
1. Pure black backgrounds save power on AMOLED and look premium
2. Card-based navigation works brilliantly on small touchscreens (Rabbit R1 proved this)
3. 2-3 accent colors max on dark -- more looks cluttered
4. Typography IS the design on dark screens -- choose one great typeface
5. Animation should communicate state (listening, thinking, responding) not just decoration
6. Widget density: 4 items visible at once is the sweet spot for 720px width
