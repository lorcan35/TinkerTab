PRINCIPLES · 01The ambient state

  • One hero, full stop. The orb is 500px — over a third of the canvas. Presence, not decoration. Mode is shown by the orb's tint (here: amber with a rose ember — Claw). Long-press orb to cycle modes.
  • No chrome. No nav bar, no input pill, no cards. A diamond marks mode; two corner labels hold system state. Everything else is breathing room.
  • Time as a word. "Afternoon" in Calistoga 88px. The numeric "14 : 32" sits below as a whisper. The device tells time the way a person would.
  • Activity as a stream. A thin vertical hairline down the middle, three nodes, three mono labels. When no agents run, the stream disappears entirely — the screen becomes even quieter.
  • A sentence, not a feed. "Earlier — you asked about AirPods. I saved the thread." One readable line replaces two info cards. The device speaks in first person; you can skim it from 3 meters away.
  • Edge ticks = affordances. Tiny 2×40px amber marks on the left and right edges hint at the two swipe gestures (left: history, right: settings). Bottom chevron hints: swipe up → focus.

PRINCIPLES · 02The focus state

  • The orb becomes the tell. On focus, the orb collapses to 120px in the upper-right corner — a live pulse showing the voice is still listening. Tap it to return to ambient.
  • Agent speaks as self. "I scanned your inbox — three replies were waiting." A Calistoga display line carries the narrative; a tiny task stream below shows the mechanical detail for those who want it.
  • No tabs. Two typographic sections: HEARTBEAT · LIVE and EARLIER TODAY. Headings are monospace hairlines, not icons. Hierarchy via type, not containers.
  • Ask is a prompt, not a field. A single amber cursor + serif italic "say something…". There's no rounded pill trying to look like a text input. It's a conversation opener.
  • Keyboard is an optional escape hatch. ⌨ or type as a secondary affordance — voice-first, text-possible.
